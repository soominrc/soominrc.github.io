4 Qualities of Color Theory

Color theory encompasses several key qualities that explain how colors interact and affect one another. Hue refers to the actual color itself, such as red, blue, or green, and can be classified as primary, secondary, or tertiary. Saturation describes the intensity or purity of a color; highly saturated colors appear vivid and bright, while desaturated colors appear more muted or grayish, influencing their emotional impact. Lastly, temperature categorizes colors as warm (reds, oranges, yellows) or cool (blues, greens, purples), affecting the mood and atmosphere of a design. Understanding these qualities allows artists and designers to create visually appealing and effective color combinations
